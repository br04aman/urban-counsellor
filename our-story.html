The Spark

A conversation that changed everything

It started in 2022, with a conversation that lasted less than ten minutes — but left a mark that never faded. A close friend confided that they had been struggling with anxiety for over a year, seeing a therapist in secret, too afraid to tell anyone because of what people might think.

That wasn't unusual. That was normal. And that was the problem.

In a country of 1.4 billion people, mental health is still whispered about in private. Therapy is something people hide from their families. Asking for help is misread as weakness. The stigma isn't just cultural — it's embedded in the language, the lunch-table conversations, the way parents respond when their children say they're "not okay."

"I didn't start Urban Counsellor to build a product. I started it because I watched someone I love carry a weight they didn't have to carry alone — and I thought: this has to be easier." — Aman Singh, Founder

The idea was simple, even if the execution wasn't: build a place where getting mental health support feels as normal — and as private — as ordering food online or booking a cab. No judgement. No paperwork. No waiting rooms.

The Journey

Two years, many rebirths

Building Urban Counsellor was not a straight line. There were weeks when the platform crashed before important user sessions. There were months when growth stalled and it felt like starting over. There were difficult decisions about pricing — because therapy needs to be affordable, but counsellors need to be paid fairly.

There were also moments of unexpected grace. A user who wrote in to say they had gone from not sleeping for three weeks to sleeping soundly — not because of some algorithm, but because of a counsellor who listened without rushing. A teenager who reached out from a city we'd never marketed to, saying they'd found us when they had no one else to turn to.

Those moments are why Urban Counsellor exists. They are also why it will keep evolving.

How we got here

2022

The idea is born

A personal conversation about stigma ignites the mission. Early research into India's mental health gap confirms what we already felt.

2023

First beta launch

We onboard our first 10 certified counsellors and launch in private beta with 200 users across 3 cities. Early feedback shapes everything.

2024

Expanding the platform

We add nutrition consultations, self-help programs, and 11 clinical mental health assessments. Our counsellor network grows to 50+.

2025

5,000 people supported

We cross our first major milestone. More importantly, our community of users tells us they feel less alone. That matters more than any number.

2026

Today — and beyond

We're building towards a future where mental healthcare in India is not an exception, but an expectation. The journey continues.

Our Philosophy

Responsible mental health care: beyond trends, back to what truly heals

There is a lot of noise in the wellness space right now — apps that gamify your mood, influencers dispensing therapy advice in 60-second videos, subscriptions that replace human connection with chatbot responses. We've thought carefully about where we fit in this landscape.

Our answer is deliberate: we use technology to make access easier, not to replace what matters. A real counsellor. A real conversation. A real commitment to your wellbeing over time.

"Alone, you may go fast. Together, you go far. With the right intent and a few thoughtful changes, it's possible to do both." — Aman Singh

We are not trying to replace therapy. We are trying to make it findable, affordable, and destigmatised — so that the next time someone needs help, reaching out feels like the obvious choice rather than a last resort.

That is the story Urban Counsellor is still writing. And you — whether you're a user, a counsellor, or someone who just stumbled onto this page at 2am — you're part of it.
